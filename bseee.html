BS - Electrical and Electronic Engineering

Thesis

MOSFET Modeling by Curve Fitting Approach and Comparison to the Ideal and Real Output Characteristics.

Abstract

The ideal MOSFET output characteristic in saturation is different from the practical output characteristic. Ideal MOSFET equations are established with a lot of assumptions, which are studied. But the SPICE simulators use lot more difficult equations to predict the real world situation of saturation curve. These equations eliminate the assumptions made for the ideal condition. Besides, the simulators use hundreds of parameters with various level of analysis. Levels in SPICE means the modeling approaches made. As an example, HSPICE has level 1 of Schichman-Hodges model to level 50 of Philips MOS9 model. We will discuss about level 49 of BSIM3 Version 3 MOSFET model here. There is also a BSIM4.6.2 available now and they never stop their analysis for a better model. These models can predict the practical output characteristics of MOSFET almost accurately. But unfortunately the equations they use are too complicated for hand calculation and it is also hard to deal with hundreds of parameters. But we want to predict the practical situation of MOSFET output characteristic by equations which is suitable for hand calculation. We will do this by curve fitting approach of Multiple Polynomial Regression Model. By this analysis, we will be able to predict the real world situation dealing with only few curve fitting parameters. The paper includes the detail analysis of curve fitting approach. Error analysis shows us how accurately curve fitting approach can predict the output characteristic. We have done the error analysis for both ideal case and curve fitting approach with the real world values.
